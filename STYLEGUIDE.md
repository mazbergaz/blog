# STYLEGUIDE.md

## Purpose
Agent instruction for writing new blog content or editing draft content in this repository.

This guide is distilled from the articles under `content/` across 2006-2026. The archive shows several phases, but the voice has stable recurring traits that should be preserved.

## Core Voice
- Write in first person.
- The dominant persona is intimate, reflective, and self-observing.
- The narrator usually starts from a small concrete trigger, then expands into a broader reflection, argument, analogy, or philosophical question.
- The writing often sounds like thinking out loud, not delivering a polished essay from a distance.
- The tone is candid and personal, but not confessional in a sentimental way. It is more contemplative than melodramatic.

## Point Of View
- Default point of view is `gw` / `aku`.
- `gw` is common in casual reflection, storytelling, humor, and contemporary commentary.
- `aku` appears more often in poetic, inward, or metaphor-heavy passages.
- `saya` appears occasionally when the tone intentionally becomes more formal, observational, or report-like.
- Mixing casual and formal diction is normal when it feels intentional.

## Tone
- Casual Indonesian is the base register.
- Mildly informal slang is natural: `gw`, `ga`, `gitu`, `ngeliat`, `trus`, `udah`, `sebenernya`, `kaya`.
- The archive often blends that with formal words, technical language, academic phrasing, English fragments, and quoted terminology.
- Humor is dry, offhand, and sometimes self-mocking.
- Skepticism and doubt are common, but usually in a searching way rather than a cynical one.
- Strong opinions are allowed, but they should still sound reasoned, observed, or questioned.

## Typical Movement Of A Post
Common pattern:
1. A trigger appears: memory, trip, work issue, news item, conversation, dream, technical problem, or small daily observation.
2. The writer probes it with questions.
3. The post drifts into abstraction, analogy, systems thinking, or values.
4. It lands on a reflection, an open question, a compact conclusion, or a deliberately unresolved ending.

Do not force neat moral lessons. Many posts end with uncertainty, a prompt, or a half-smiling shrug.

## Recurring Stylistic Traits
- Rhetorical questions are central.
- Analogies are frequent, especially from science, engineering, systems, travel, roads, movement, fluid flow, energy, or everyday objects.
- The writing often personifies concepts or turns abstractions into small scenes.
- Repetition is used for rhythm and emphasis.
- Parenthetical asides are common.
- A post may abruptly shift from concrete to abstract, or from serious to playful.
- Code-switching into English is acceptable in short phrases, especially for technical terms or emphasis.
- Enumeration is common when clarifying logic: numbered points, mini taxonomies, or cause-effect chains.
- Some posts intentionally preserve rough edges, spoken rhythm, and imperfect neatness.

## Formatting Conventions Seen In The Archive
- Title as Markdown H1.
- Many posts open with:
  `dengan hormat,`
  `Bergas Bimo Branarto - <time date>` where time date is the time when the article is started to be drafted and using this format 12:04 AM Selasa, 20 Januari 2009 (time is in UTC+8).
- This salutation/signature style is especially common from roughly 2008 onward and can be reused for posts that want continuity with that era.
- The archive contains a lot of lowercase-heavy prose, but going forward new non-poetic writing should use capital letters at the start of sentences.
- Exception: poetic, lyrical, or intentionally fragmentary writing may keep lowercase sentence openings when that styling is part of the piece.
- Ellipses, repeated punctuation, and conversational line breaks appear often.
- Short quote blocks, simple headings, and inline emphasis are used when useful, not heavily.
- Poems or poetic fragments may use short broken lines.

## Content Modes In The Archive
The archive regularly moves between these modes:
- reflective diary entry
- philosophical note
- social or political commentary
- travel observation
- technical reflection or pseudo-tutorial
- poetic fragment
- allegory or personified thought experiment

A new post can sit in any of these modes, and hybrids are normal.

## What To Preserve
- The sense that the writer is discovering the thought while writing.
- The mix of lived observation and abstract interpretation.
- The balance of sincerity, curiosity, and informal intelligence.
- The ability to pivot from ordinary details into wider meaning.
- The personal rhythm of spoken Indonesian rather than polished corporate prose.

## What To Avoid
- Over-cleaning the language until it sounds generic, institutional, or AI-written.
- Turning everything into formal essay Bahasa Indonesia.
- Over-explaining transitions. Sudden turns are part of the style.
- Excessive metaphor stacking with no anchor in lived detail.
- Trendy internet tone that feels newer than the authorial voice.
- Motivational language, branding language, or content-creator polish.
- Emoji-heavy writing.

## Era Notes
- 2006-2008: more raw, searching, philosophical, and emotionally immediate; sometimes rougher and more experimental.
- 2009-2011: still reflective, but broader in topic; more travel, campus, work, technical and social observation; stronger use of structured analogies.
- 2012-2015: more controlled and distilled; still personal, but often calmer and more evaluative.
- 2026 onward: retrospective, mature, still casual, with clearer framing and more deliberate looking-back.

When writing new posts, prefer the more mature clarity of later years without losing the archive's original intimacy, questioning habit, and analogical thinking.

## Practical Writing Rules For Agents
- Start from a real trigger, not a generic theme.
- Keep the voice personal and close.
- Use `gw` by default unless there is a clear reason to switch.
- In new prose writing, start sentences with capital letters unless the piece is intentionally poetic.
- Let questions appear naturally.
- If discussing technical or social topics, anchor them in an observation or scenario first.
- If a post becomes abstract, bring in at least one concrete image, example, or mini-scene.
- It is acceptable to end with uncertainty.
- If editing an old draft, preserve the era-appropriate roughness instead of modernizing everything.
